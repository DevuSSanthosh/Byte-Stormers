Exercises to Do During Your Period

Regular exercise during your period can help alleviate cramps, boost your mood, and increase overall well-being. Here are some exercises that are generally safe and beneficial during menstruation:

1. Walking

A brisk walk can help improve circulation, reduce bloating, and ease cramps. Aim for 30 minutes of walking each day.

Safety note: Start with a comfortable pace and distance, gradually increasing as you feel able.

2. Yoga

Gentle yoga poses can help stretch tense muscles and promote relaxation. Focus on poses like Child's Pose, Cat-Cow, and Supine Twist.

Safety note: Avoid inverted poses during heavy flow days. Listen to your body and don't push yourself too hard.

3. Swimming

The buoyancy of water can help relieve cramps and the gentle resistance can improve circulation. It's also a great full-body workout.

Safety note: Use a tampon or menstrual cup while swimming. If you're not comfortable, try other exercises.

4. Pilates

Pilates exercises can help strengthen your core, which may alleviate back pain associated with menstruation.

Safety note: Focus on gentle, controlled movements. Avoid exercises that put too much pressure on your abdomen if you're experiencing cramps.

5. Light Strength Training

Using light weights or resistance bands can help maintain muscle strength and may boost your mood through endorphin release.

Safety note: Use lighter weights than usual and focus on proper form. Stop if you feel dizzy or experience increased pain.

6. Stretching

Gentle stretching can help relieve muscle tension and promote relaxation. Focus on stretches for your lower back, hips, and thighs.

Safety note: Stretch gently and avoid bouncing. Hold each stretch for 15-30 seconds without straining.

General Tips for Exercising During Your Period

  • Listen to your body and adjust the intensity as needed.
  • Stay hydrated before, during, and after exercise.
  • Wear comfortable, breathable clothing.
  • Use appropriate menstrual products that allow for comfortable movement.
  • Don't push yourself too hard - it's okay to take it easy if you're not feeling up to intense exercise.
